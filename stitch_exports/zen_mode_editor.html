Chapter V: The Sword in the Stone

The snow lay thick and undisturbed in the churchyard, blanketing the ancient graves in a silence so profound it felt heavy. Arthur pulled his cloak tighter against the biting wind, his breath pluming in the crisp morning air. He had been sent to fetch Sir Kay's sword, a simple errand, yet the pavilion was locked, and the tournament was about to begin. Panic, cold and sharp as the frost, gnawed at him.

It was then he saw it. Rising from the center of the courtyard, surrounded by a halo of undisturbed snow, was a massive block of dark marble. Anvil-shaped, it seemed to drink the pale winter light. And thrust deep into its heart was a sword.

The hilt was ornate, wrapped in worn leather and bound with gold wire that caught a solitary ray of breaking sun. The pommel held a gemstone that seemed to pulse with a dormant, subterranean fire. Letters were etched deep into the stone block beneath it, though Arthur, in his haste, did not stop to decipher them. He needed a weapon for his brother. This one was abandoned, forgotten.

He approached, his footsteps the only sound in the vast, white stillness. He reached out, his gloved hand closing around the cold grip. As his fingers tightened, a strange sensation washed over him—not a shock, but a deep, resonant hum that vibrated through his bones, like the tolling of a distant, massive bell. He pulled.

It did not catch. It did not grate against the stone. The blade slid free as smoothly as if it had been resting in oiled leather. Arthur stumbled back slightly, the weight of the magnificent weapon perfect in his hand, the air suddenly thick with an unspoken destiny.
