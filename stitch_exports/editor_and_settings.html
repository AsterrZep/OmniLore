The Sword in the Stone

The dawn broke over the frost-kissed moors of Albion, casting long, violet shadows across the ancient courtyard. In its center, an anvil sat atop a jagged monolith, and thrust deep into the iron was a blade of indeterminate origin. It pulsed with a faint, otherworldly luminescence—a quiet hum that resonated in the marrow of anyone who stood too close.

Arthur, shivering beneath a threadbare cloak that offered little protection against the biting wind, approached the stone. The gathering crowd had fallen silent, their breaths pluming in the frigid air like ghostly apparitions. Knights of renowned lineage had tried and failed, their gauntleted hands slipping from the hilt, their pride fractured against the immovable steel.
